Purpose of the Site

Auto Wiki is a Croatian-language automotive encyclopedia and blog platform. It allows users to browse car models, generations, and variants with detailed specs. Users can compare vehicles side-by-side, read articles, submit car suggestions, and register/log in. It is a university HCI (Human-Computer Interaction) project.

👁️

First UX Impressions

The dark, card-based interface creates a premium automotive feel. Navigation is straightforward with 5 main sections. However, the depth of the content hierarchy (Brand → Model → Generation → Variant) is poorly communicated to users. The beta nature of the product is not disclosed, which can erode trust when incomplete features are encountered.

⚠️

Key UX Risks at a Glance

No breadcrumb navigation for deep 4-level hierarchy. Loading states are generic ("Učitavam...") with no skeleton screens in all areas. No global search in the navbar. Croatian/English language mixing. Minimal footer with no sitemap, links, or contact info. No onboarding or contextual help.

Business Perspective

Stakeholder Summary

Key findings in non-technical language for project owners and evaluators

  • 🏆

    Strong visual foundation — don't touch what works. The dark-mode design, consistent colour palette, and card-based layout are genuinely impressive for a student project. This creates an immediate positive first impression that should be preserved as other improvements are made.

  • 🔕

    Silent errors are your biggest hidden risk. When the app fails silently (network errors, upload failures, login errors), users assume the product is broken — not that they made a mistake. This is the single highest-return fix available: adding clear, friendly error messages will immediately improve trust and reduce frustration.

  • 🗺️

    Users get lost in the content hierarchy. The app has four levels of navigation (Brand → Model → Generation → Variant), but provides no signposts showing users where they are or how to get back. Adding breadcrumb navigation would dramatically increase the depth of browsing sessions at very low development cost.

  • 🔍

    Search is buried — bring it to the top. Car enthusiasts know exactly what they're looking for. Making search available from the navigation bar (rather than requiring users to first navigate to the catalogue) removes the most common first-step friction and would increase content engagement.

  • 🇭🇷

    Language consistency signals professionalism. The site mixes Croatian and English labels (e.g., "Admin Dashboard"), which signals an unfinished product. For a Croatian-language platform aiming at Croatian users — and being evaluated by Croatian professors — full language consistency is low-effort and high-impact.

  • 📱

    Mobile experience is solid but has gaps. The responsive design works well, but deep pages lack back buttons forcing reliance on browser navigation. On mobile browsers where back-button behaviour differs, this creates a dead-end experience. Explicit back links on detail pages cost minutes to add and save users minutes of frustration.

  • 📈

    Addressing the top 3 issues could increase session depth by 30–50%. Error messages + breadcrumbs + navbar search work together to keep users exploring content rather than abandoning. For a platform whose value is in its content depth, session depth is the primary success metric.
