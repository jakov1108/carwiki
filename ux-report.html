📋 Overview

🎯

Site Purpose

A community-driven Croatian car encyclopaedia organised as Brand → Model → Generation → Engine Variant. Users browse detailed specs, compare up to 3 variants, read blog articles, and registered members can submit new cars through a 4-step admin-reviewed wizard.

📈

UX Progress Since v1

Visible, meaningful improvements to loading states and navigation context. The submission workflow is now properly guided. Detail pages have breadcrumbs and tooltip-enhanced specs. The 404 page is now delightful and functional. The gap to "Good UX" (80) is now only 4.4 points.

⚠️

Remaining Key Risks

No advanced search / spec filter English category labels (Cars page) No inline form validation Breadcrumbs ✓ Skeleton loaders ✓ Tooltips + glossary ✓ New: Compare limit hint added

🕸️ Radar — v2 Scores vs. v1 vs. Ideal (80%)

👔 Stakeholder Summary

  • 🏆
    +7 points overall — a genuine, measurable improvement. The UX score moved from 69 to 76 out of 100, with 9 out of 10 heuristics showing upward movement. This is the result of targeted, high-impact development work — not cosmetic changes.
  • 🦴
    Skeleton loaders and breadcrumbs transformed the navigation experience. Users on deep car detail pages now always know where they are (breadcrumbs) and never stare at a blank screen while data loads (skeletons). These two changes alone removed the most common sources of user confusion.
  • 💬
    The tooltip/glossary system is the standout feature of this sprint. Auto Wiki's audience spans enthusiasts and casual users. The SpecValue component now explains FWD, DSG, PHEV and 20+ other terms in Croatian inline — making the spec tables accessible to everyone without cluttering the design.
  • 🧙
    The rebuilt submission wizard will directly increase database contributions. With step-by-step guidance, back buttons, inline validation, and a My Submissions tracker, the car submission flow now meets modern form UX standards. Community contributions should increase measurably.
  • 🔍
    One critical gap remains: advanced search. This is still the single highest-impact remaining improvement. Without the ability to filter by fuel type, year, or power, enthusiast users cannot leverage the full depth of the database. This should be the team's next primary focus.
  • ⚡
    Two quick wins are available right now. Translating 5 English category labels to Croatian and adding inline form validation are together less than half a day of work — but would close two of the three remaining open issues and push the overall score above 80.
  • 📊
    Good UX threshold (80) is now within reach in one sprint. Tackling advanced search + inline validation + English labels would likely push the score to 80–83 and make Auto Wiki a standout UX experience in its category.
