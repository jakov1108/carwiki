Executive Summary

Third-generation evaluation — all three sprint results compared

What this report covers

This is the third heuristic evaluation of Auto Wiki (carwikibeta.onrender.com), a Croatian automotive encyclopedia built with React + TypeScript + PostgreSQL for the FESB HCI course. The evaluation applies Nielsen's 10 usability heuristics, scoring each 0–100.

Key milestone: v3 reaches an overall score of 81.0, crossing the "Good UX" threshold of 80 for the first time. The most significant gains this sprint came from Help & Documentation (+11), Match/Real World (+7), Error Prevention (+6), and Error Recovery (+6).

  • Overall score improved from 75.6 (v2) to 81.0 (v3) — a gain of +5.4 points.
  • Two heuristics now score 86–87: User Control & Freedom and Recognition vs. Recall.
  • Error Prevention (74) remains the only heuristic below 75 — inline form validation is the clearest remaining gap.
  • Flexibility & Efficiency (70) is the second-lowest but improved significantly (+8) due to deep-links and slug URLs.

Top Remaining Priorities

Issues still open — ranked by UX impact and implementation effort

🔴 High

Spec-based search / filter on Cars & Models pages

No way to filter by fuel type, power range, or year. A user looking for "all diesel cars under 120 kW" must scan manually. This is the single biggest remaining gap — directly addressed by Heuristic 7 (Flexibility). A filter sidebar or inline dropdowns above the grid would fix this.

Heuristic 7 Effort: medium
🔴 High

Inline real-time form validation on Register & Login

Currently both forms only validate on submit. Password length, email format, and "required" errors are only shown after the button is pressed. Adding onBlur or onChange validation with inline error messages under each field would fix Heuristic 5 (Error Prevention).

Heuristic 5 Effort: low

🎓 Academic Summary — March 12, 2026

Auto Wiki has reached a UX score of 81.0 / 100 — crossing the "Good UX" threshold (80) for the first time. This is the result of three focused improvement sprints over approximately six weeks, lifting the score from a baseline of 68.6.

The most significant v3 improvements this sprint are the ConfirmDialog system (prevents accidental destructive actions), the About page FAQ and Glossary (reduces cognitive load for non-technical users), contextual login/register error messages (guides users toward recovery rather than leaving them confused), and the blog carousel with touch support (improves mobile engagement).

The three remaining priorities are: (1) spec-based search/filter — the most impactful missing feature; (2) inline form validation on auth pages — a low-effort, high-impact win; and (3) localising the English category labels — a trivial one-line change. Completing even one of these would push the score above 82.
